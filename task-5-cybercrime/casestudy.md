# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud (QR Code Scam)

This case study examines a prevalent form of digital financial crime in India: the QR Code
"Receive Money" Scam. While the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is highly secure,
fraudsters exploit user psychology through social engineering.

## What is the Crime?

UPI/Online Payment Fraud involves deceiving a victim into authorizing a digital transaction
that results in a loss of funds. In this specific variation, the scammer convinces the victim that
they must scan a QR code or enter their UPI PIN to receive a payment, whereas the technology
is designed such that a PIN/scan is only required to send money.

## Realistic Fictional Scenario: The "Marketplace" Trap

The Victim: Rohan, a college student selling his old mountain bike on an online marketplace
(like OLX or Facebook Marketplace).

### Step-by-Step Modus Operandi:

1. Targeting: A "buyer" (the scammer) contacts Rohan within minutes of his post,
expressing urgent interest and agreeing to the full price without negotiation to build
excitement.

2. Building Trust: The scammer claims to be a busy professional or an army officer who
cannot meet in person but wants to "reserve" the bike by paying an advance.

3. The Bait: The scammer sends a QR code via WhatsApp, telling Rohan: "Scan this code to
receive the ₹5,000 advance in your account."

4. The Execution: When Rohan scans the code using his UPI app, a payment screen
appears. In his haste and excitement, he doesn't notice the screen says "Pay ₹5,000"
instead of "Receive." He enters his UPI PIN. +1

5. The Loss: Instantly, ₹5,000 is debited from Rohan's account. The scammer immediately
blocks him and disappears.

## Targets and Consequences
### Who is Targeted? 
Anyone using digital marketplaces, small-scale vendors, or individuals
less familiar with the technical nuances of UPI. Scammers often target those displaying a
sense of urgency.

### Consequences:

. Financial Loss: Victims often lose amounts ranging from a few thousand to several
lakhs of rupees.

. Emotional Distress: Victims feel a sense of violation and embarrassment due to the
"self-authorized" nature of the fraud.

. Legal/Banking Hurdles: Since the victim technically authorized the payment with
their PIN, banks often find it difficult to reverse the transaction unless reported
within the "Golden Hour" (the first two hours).

Important Note: You never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive
money. If someone asks you to do so, it is 100% a scam.
